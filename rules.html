Rules

  1. Match Structure
    • Objective: The main objective is to pin the opponent's shoulders to the mat.
    • Duration: Matches are typically divided into periods, with the total match time varying by level and type of competition.
  2. Scoring
    • Takedown:Bringing the opponent to the mat from a standing position (worth 3 points).
    • Escape:Getting away from the opponent's control and returning to a standing position (worth 1 point).
    • Reversal:Reversing control from the bottom position to the top position (worth 2 points).
    • Near Fall:Holding the opponent's shoulders near the mat (worth 2, 3, or 4 points, depending on the duration).
  3. Illegal Moves
    • No striking, punching, or kicking.
    • No choking or using pressure points.
    • No pulling of hair, clothing, or headgear.
    • No locking of joints in a way that could cause injury.
  4. Conduct
    • Wrestlers must display good sportsmanship at all times.
    • Any unsportsmanlike conduct, such as taunting or excessive celebration, can result in penalties.
  5. Out-of-Bounds
    • Wrestlers are considered out-of-bounds when any part of their body is outside the designated wrestling area.
    • The referee will stop the match and restart it from the center.
  6. Winning the Match
    • Pin:The match ends immediately if a wrestler pins the opponent's shoulders to the mat.
    • Technical Fall:A wrestler wins if they gain a certain lead in points over the opponent (typically 15 points).
    • Decision:The wrestler with the most points at the end of the match wins.
    • Default: A wrestler wins if the opponent cannot continue due to injury or other reasons.
    • Disquallification:A wrestler wins if the opponent is disqualified for repeated violations.
  7. Equipment and Attire
    • Wrestlers must wear a singlet, wrestling shoes, and headgear.
    • No jewelry or accessories are allowed.
  8. Recent rule changes of 2024

    • Imbounds with One point of Contact:Only one point of contact by either wrestler is required to be inbounds.
